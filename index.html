Introduction

Who I Am

The field of programming appealed to me as a young girl; back then, the extent of my programming experience was using HTML on my Neopets' pages to give them a personalized feel.

I discovered HTML when I came across a website providing visitors simple HTML code that changed the display of their webpage. At the age of 9, I didn't know about the copy and paste feature on a computer, so I wrote down every line of code to type back into my blank space until I discovered this much more efficient function!

The years have honed my professional skills, and I'm leagues away from the pen-and-paper transcribing of background colors and hyperlinks. Now, I aspire to bring my sharpened skills to the workplace, bringing value to any company with my professional and personal excellence.

Where I'm Located

I am currently residing in beautiful Phoenix, Arizona with my family!

About Me

Passion for Development

My passion for programming as a career stemmed from my time in the classroom as a public school teacher. As a way of offering our low-income community options other than a traditional 4-year college degree, the district adopted a basic coding curriculum to inspire students to pursue computer science or software development as a career. Inadvertently through these lessons, the idea became more appealing to me for my own future, and I quit my job as a teacher to pursue it full time. This unique journey into coding has lead me to appreciate the creativity, flexibility, and endless possibilities that the career brings. In the future, I look forward to building an app that will help educators track classroom management concerns!

Community is a requirement for any field; building a circle of mentors, cheerleaders, and empathetic friends is imperitive for success in a field.

  1. Mentors: Sage advice from experts ensures that I can grow and become the best developer possible.
  2. Cheerleaders: Various people who know my character can attest to my perseverence and encourage me to continue when situations get challenging.
  3. Empathetic Friends: Traveling the new road with other beginners can lead to an empathetic understanding of my struggle, my successes, and my fears.

With strong community and personal character traits, I am excited to thrive in this new career and work with others to bring about their successes as well!
